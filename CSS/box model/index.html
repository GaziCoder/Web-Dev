Understanding the Box Model

The box model is a fundamental concept in web design and development that describes how elements are structured and displayed on a webpage.

Components of the Box Model

  • Content: The actual content of the box, such as text or images.
  • Padding: The space between the content and the border, which creates breathing room around the content.
  • Border: A line that wraps around the padding (if any) and content.
  • Margin: The outermost layer that creates space between this box and other boxes.

Margin collapse

Margin collapse ek CSS ka feature he jo vertical margins ke beech me space ko control karta he.

Jab do block-level elements vertically stacked hote he, toh unke margins automatically adjust ho jate he aur unke beech me space kam ho jata he.

Jab do box upar niche hote he toh unki margins overlap karti he. hamesha dono boxes ke beech comparatively badi margin ke box ki space hogi.

For example, agar do boxes ke beech margin 20px aur 30px he toh dono boxes ke beech 30px ki space hogi.

Isse margin collapse kehte he. Iska matlab ye nahi ki dono boxes ke beech 50px ki space hogi.

Margin collapse sirf vertical margin ke liye hota he. Horizontal margin collapse nahi hota.

Margin collapse ka use karne se layout ko control karna asaan hota he aur unwanted space se bachne me madad milti he.
